\section{Introduction}
The term "Research Software Engineer" has now been around for over 10 years. In the time since it was first "created" back in 2012, we have seen many developments in how people undertaking software engineering are recognised and supported within the research community. However, these advances have largely followed slightly different pathways in different countries. In Germany this has been represented through the creation, growth and formalisation of the deRSE community, representing people building and maintaining research software across a range of roles. In the German research community, particularly within universities, the job title "Research Software Engineer" is still fairly unusual - many of the people developing research software have roles that classify them as researchers or academics.

In this BoF session, we looked at current career structures for RSEs in Germany and also consider international developments in RSE. We then looked at what we, as a community, see as the most important areas where the RSE role can and should be developed in Germany, and how we think career pathways for research software professionals can be improved.

The key things that we hoped to get out of this BoF session are:

\begin{itemize}

\item An improved understanding of the challenges RSEs have or the concerns they face around career progression.

\item Community perspectives on suitable career pathways for technical roles.

\item Approaches for engaging with key stakeholders to promote greater awareness and recognition of the importance of supporting technical roles.
\end{itemize}

We see this development of career pathways for RSEs as a long-term challenge for our community
and we therefore hope that this will be a session that we can run annually at deRSE.
By cataloguing this year's fears and hopes we can review progress in the next year and look at how we can catalyse further change.\\

The workshop was performed in three rounds of fish-bowl with roughly 20 minuntes each. First we started by focusing on
how do RSEs see themselves, and how are they seen by others?
The second round considered a more outwards looking perspective, what they feel is missing, and which opportunities they see.
And in the last round we asked about systemic changes that are required.

\section{Summary}
RSEs consider their tasks as important and really like to perform well in their tasks. But the acceptance of the name and a lack
of promotion hinders acceptance. Participants feel that the societies could do more to promote RSE in the universities,
while a general trend reversal to ``Make Mittelbau Great Again!'' is rallied for by the participants.
