## Introduction
In this BoF session, we will look at current career structures for RSEs in Germany and also consider international developments in RSE.
We'll then look at what we, as a community, see as the most important areas where the RSE role can and should be developed in Germany, and how we think career pathways for research software professionals can be improved.
The session will begin with a group of short talks by invited people for around 25 minutes to set the scene.

Have your smartphones ready!

### Fishbowl rules
In this model, we’ll begin with a group of four invited panellists but also 1 empty seat.
As the discussion gets going, we’ll invite an audience member with points that
they’d like to contribute to join the panel taking the empty seat. Going forward,
we’ll continue to invite audience members to join the panel with existing panel
members stepping down and returning to the audience.
This approach will give us the opportunity to hear a wide range of perspectives
and input in an equitable and ﬂexible manner.

### Professional Identity and Perception
- Are you in an RSE Position or a domain position?
- What is your professional identity? Do you describe yourself more as a "scientist who codes", a software engineer, a computer scientist, an actual RSE, none of the above? Are you happy with your professional identity?
 - How are your perceived in your institution? (status, granted autonomy, correct perception of what you are doing, ...). Are you happy with this situation?
 - Is your carreer prospect and your evaluation aligned with what you perceive as "doing a good job"? If not, how could the situation be improved?
 - Share what you like and dislike in your current position.
 - Does your job depend on the continued acquisition of project funding?
 - Do you see a career possibility in your current position?

### Perspectives on career pathways for technical roles
Community perspectives on suitable career pathways for technical roles.

- What is missing for you looking forward?
- How can your career be better supported?
- Is there a career in industry?
- Does your institution support your professional development and career? (Note: negative support exists…)
- How did you promote RSE in your organization?

### What systemic changes are needed to support RSE careers?

- What would an ideal academic career structure for RSEs look like in 4 – 8 years, and what changes are needed now to move toward it?
- What can professional societies (i.e. de-RSE e.V.) do to support RSE career development?
- Would it help to better represent and credit the role of software in academic evaluation systems? Or should the work of RSEs rather not be tied to traditional measures of academic achievement?
- Would it help to establish central RSE units in research institutions?
- Do you know of something internationally that you would like to see implemented in Germany?
- Which institutions, boards, people should be approached to promote necessary institutional changes with regard to RSE carreer pathways and how?
